Brockton School Zones

Authored by: Jillian Riseman

I grew up in Brockton, Massachusetts, the largest Massachusetts city outside of the Metro Boston Area. The city is historically underserved immigrant and low-income communities, lacking in funding and access to resources. For this week's project, I mapped a 1-mile buffer around each data point labled "school", to highlight the public parks and other amenities that are located within school-zones. It is statistically important for urban areas to have equitable access to green spaces to provide enrichment opportunities for residents, and from this map, you can see that public parks are not evenly distributed around this city. Many of Brockton's parks are very small and clustered in the center of the city, with a few larger green spaces in the suburbs. There are very few parks in the north, east, and south, which can contribute to environmental issues like the increased temperatures and flooding that many neighborhoods experience.

Data Sources from ESRI Standard Open Street Maps and images sourced from Wikipedia and Flickr.com.


This map used only data sourced from ESRI Standard OSM, which may not be a fully accurate representation of every single amenity or school in Brockton, MA.
